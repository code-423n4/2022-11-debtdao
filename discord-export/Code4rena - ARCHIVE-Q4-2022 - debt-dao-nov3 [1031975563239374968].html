Would depositAndClose always close the first line ?
Bob 11/02/2022 08:52
depositAndClose() allows a Borrower to close an individual credit line. The FIFO repayment queue ensures that credit lines are repaid (partially or fully/closed) in the order in which they were originally drawn down by a Borrower. So whenever a Borrower deposits to repay, those funds are allocated to the first credit line (identified by an 'id') in the queue.

Koolex
When speaking about a Line of Credit, What's the difference between insolvent and impaired? I understood that when it is insolvent , it is liquidation failed to be done. what could be a reason of this?
Bob 11/04/2022 16:41
As it's coded today, the status of a Line of Credit can be one of uninitialized, active, liquidatable, repaid or insolvent. Impaired is not an official status but obviously if that word is used it means there's a problem, a doubt that full repayment can be made of principal and interest. Starting in reverse with the INSOLVENT status, this will be declared by an Arbiter if a Borrower is permanently incapable of repaying debt AFTER actions were taken when the status is LIQUIDATABLE. If the status is LIQUIDATABLE (for whatever reason), then there are 3 ways a Lender can recover funds (depending on what contracts are deployed/what terms agreed between parties). We've an explainer on this here: https://docs.debtdao.finance/products/line-of-credit/loan-impairment-and-lender-recourse If you need further assistance, please ask.
